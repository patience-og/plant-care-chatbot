# Kenyan & Meru Local Plants

The Hibiscus is a popular flowering plant in Kenya.  
Hibiscus plants need full sunlight and regular watering.  
To make your Hibiscus bloom, prune old branches regularly.  
Hibiscus flowers attract bees and butterflies.  
Water Hibiscus deeply once or twice a week.  
Hibiscus leaves turning yellow may indicate overwatering.  
Fertilize Hibiscus monthly during growing season.  
Hibiscus is sensitive to cold temperatures.  

Bougainvillea thrives in sunny locations.  
Bougainvillea requires well-drained soil.  
Prune Bougainvillea after flowering to promote new growth.  
Fertilize Bougainvillea monthly during the growing season.  
Bougainvillea can tolerate dry conditions better than excessive water.  
Bougainvillea flowers need at least 6 hours of sunlight daily.  
Water Bougainvillea only when the soil is dry.  
Bougainvillea stems are thorny; handle with care.  

The African Violet grows well indoors.  
African Violets need bright, indirect sunlight.  
Keep soil moist but not soggy for African Violets.  
Do not get water on the leaves of African Violets.  
Fertilize African Violets every two weeks with a balanced fertilizer.  
Repot African Violets every 12-18 months.  
African Violets bloom better with proper humidity.  
Remove dead flowers to encourage new blooms.  

The Spider Plant is easy to care for indoors.  
Spider Plants prefer bright, indirect sunlight.  
Water Spider Plants when the soil is dry to the touch.  
Spider Plants remove toxins from indoor air.  
Propagate Spider Plants by planting their baby shoots.  
Yellow leaf tips on Spider Plants indicate over-fertilization.  
Spider Plants grow well in hanging baskets.  
Spider Plants tolerate occasional neglect.  

The Aloe Vera plant is drought-tolerant.  
Aloe Vera requires well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.  
Water Aloe Vera only when the soil is completely dry.  
Aloe Vera leaves store water, so overwatering can harm them.  
Use Aloe Vera gel for minor burns and skin care.  
Aloe Vera can be propagated by separating offsets.  
Aloe Vera thrives in pots with drainage holes.  
Aloe Vera prefers warm indoor temperatures.

Snake Plants tolerate low light and irregular watering.  
Do not overwater Snake Plants to avoid root rot.

Peace Lilies bloom indoors with proper care.  
Peace Lilies need indirect sunlight and consistent moisture.

Peace Lilies are toxic if ingested by pets.  
Peace Lilies thrive in high humidity environments.  
Peace Lilies need regular dusting of leaves.

Fiddle Leaf Fig needs bright, indirect sunlight.  
Water Fiddle Leaf Fig when the top inch of soil is dry.  
Rotate Fiddle Leaf Fig to ensure even growth.  
Dust the leaves of Fiddle Leaf Fig regularly.  
Avoid drafts and sudden temperature changes for Fiddle Leaf Fig.  
Fiddle Leaf Fig may drop leaves if overwatered.  
Use well-drained soil for Fiddle Leaf Fig.  
Fiddle Leaf Fig benefits from occasional fertilization.  

Pothos is easy to grow indoors.  
Pothos tolerates low light conditions.  
Water Pothos when the top 2 inches of soil are dry.  
Trim Pothos vines to control growth.  
Pothos can be grown in water or soil.  
Pothos improves indoor air quality.  
Pothos is safe for hanging baskets and shelves.  
Pothos propagates easily from cuttings.  

# Flowering Plants

Orchids need indirect sunlight and humidity.  
Water Orchids once a week, allowing drainage.  
Orchid flowers can last for months with proper care.  
Use orchid-specific fertilizer monthly.  
Repot Orchids every 1-2 years to refresh soil.  
Orchids need good air circulation.  
Trim dead roots and leaves to maintain Orchid health.  
Avoid overwatering Orchids to prevent root rot.  

Geraniums need at least 6 hours of sunlight daily.  
Water Geraniums when the soil feels dry.  
Deadhead Geranium flowers to promote more blooms.  
Geraniums can survive in pots or garden beds.  
Fertilize Geraniums every 4-6 weeks during growth.  
Geraniums repel certain insects in gardens.  
Geranium leaves turning yellow may indicate nutrient deficiency.  
Geraniums grow well in well-drained soil.  

Marigolds thrive in sunny gardens.  
Water Marigolds regularly but avoid waterlogging.  
Deadhead Marigolds to encourage continuous flowering.  
Marigolds repel certain pests like nematodes.  
Marigolds bloom best in fertile soil.  
Marigold flowers attract pollinators.  
Marigolds can be grown in pots or beds.  
Marigolds are low-maintenance flowering plants.  

# Troubleshooting & Common Care

Yellow leaves indicate overwatering or nutrient deficiency.  
Brown leaf tips indicate dry air or inconsistent watering.  
Wilting leaves may indicate underwatering.  
Pests like aphids or spider mites can harm plants.  
Treat pests with neem oil or insecticidal soap.  
Leaf curling can indicate too much sun exposure.  
Slow growth may indicate poor soil or lack of nutrients.  
Fungal infections appear as spots on leaves.
